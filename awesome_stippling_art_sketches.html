Awesome Stippling Art Sketches

  • January 22, 2019
  • By Dumebi Iwuchukwu

Stippling technique is a very contemporary style of drawing with dabbing and dots. Pen or brush of a single color is utilized and the ink’s density is depended on the style one wants to depict. This is similar to—but distinct from—pointillism, which uses dots of different colours to simulate blended colours

These are some amazing stippling art sketches by French artist, Hello Shane who is an illustrator, hand-letter and graffiti artist.
Building up shading in a complex piece using hundreds of tiny ink dots is a difficult technique that requires time and patience. It is also important to be able to constantly zoom in an out, to see the image on a minuscule scale, along with the full picture.

Though it may not be clear from the images, Shane primarily draws in the traditional way, using a black pen, or a pencil. He might add colour in Photoshop, but the core of the illustration is hand drawn. His grasp of his tools is impressive, such mastery requires not only talent, but a whole lot offhand work and practice too.

Shane’s work has graced posters, t-shirts and pages of clients such as Nike, Puma and Vanity Fair. He also consistently works on personal projects. Shane’s personal work adopts a more distinctive style, drawing strong influences from comic books and graphic novels. His style overall is a curious mix of vintage typography, tattoo art and comics, with a bit of Felix the Cat and Popeye of the 20s era thrown in. It’s the unique way that the illustrator has merged a range influences that makes his work special.
