My YALI Story
…Enroute to ASCON.

  • April 10, 2018
  • By Dumebi Iwuchukwu

As I alighted from my trekking device (Lag Yellow Cab) my trek coordinator (driver) looking at my boxes, asked as I paid him; “where are you flying to?”
I don’t lie.
I don’t like being embarrassed either.
Guess what I replied.

I’m going for a training I said, my people are flying in to join me. We’ll be going together.
As he ‘trekked’ away. I moved towards MM2 lobby carefully avoiding the polite porters (may I help with your bag Sir).

I was looking for anything YALI, Limo, entourage, banner, Uniformed officer with a placard, anything. Then I saw two familiar faces (thank God for our WhatsApp group). The Pilot and the Brand ambassador. Yali is coming for us they said, let’s wait in the lounge they said. We went up to the lounge and waited and waited and waited. Enugu folks arrived and I think police held Abuja plane at the airport because the ‘papers were not complete’. After a lot of talk and ₦20 they were released and finally arrived.. We entered the YALI Limo (cough) and our Journey to ASCON Topo Badagry began. An hour into our trip we got stuck in the mud at an amazing stretch of road twice. Took an Ìgbo alaye and a trailer to tow us out. Then the real journey began. As if we were going to South Africa by road. Finally, accompanied by the Agrotech Finance geek’s countdown we arrive ASCON, I was too tired to smile. Yet I smile when……. To be continued Taking my elephant for a walk…
